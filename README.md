# A Designer's POV on Gen AI & the Creative Industry

### Built with — and about — the tools reshaping how we work.

---

This is a case study and industry perspective built around **Trip Atlas**, a generative AI web application for photographers and visual travelers. It was designed and built independently, outside of any employer, as a live experiment in what it means to build natively in the Gen AI era.

The deck covers two things simultaneously:

**The industry moment** — how Generative AI is disrupting the creative industry in a way that's categorically different from previous disruptions. Not just what tools can do, but what it means for how designers, photographers, developers, and creatives work — and what they're responsible for that AI cannot replicate.

**The build** — what actually happened when one designer ran 8 structured experiments across AI tools, then used those findings to build and ship a real product, test it in the field across four cities, and start the next one.

---

## What's in here

- A historical frame: where Gen AI sits in the arc of creative industry disruption
- The shift from integrated suites to specialized agents — and what that means for workflow
- 8 pre-build experiments across UXPilot, v0, Figma Make, Lovable, and Claude — testing output quality, accessibility, design system architecture, and product ideation
- The Trip Atlas build: Claude → v0 → GitHub → production, 150+ commits, 100+ deployments
- Lessons learned — the ones you only get by building, not by reading about it
- What's next

## Trip Atlas

Trip Atlas is a generative AI web application that curates photography spots in cities you're headed to — replacing hours of pre-trip research with a single, structured output. It was used in the field across Edinburgh, Amsterdam, Barcelona, and Dublin.

The app is currently in beta.
